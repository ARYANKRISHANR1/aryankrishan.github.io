Navigating the Charge: A Strategic Audit of India’s EV Ecosystem

Overview

Derived from an in-depth seminar led by industry veteran Mr. Sushil Reddy, this project examines the rapid growth and systemic limitations of India's EV sector. While the number of EV charging stations is experiencing exponential growth, significant infrastructure and consumer experience gaps remain. The study balances the long-term environmental benefits of EVs against the immediate logistical hurdles that prevent mass-market transition.

My Role & What I Did

  • Infrastructure Analysis: Evaluated the rapid expansion of EV charging networks while identifying critical limitations, such as uneven geographic distribution and grid instability.
  • Comparative Lifecycle Audit: Conducted a CO2 emissions comparison between internal combustion engines (Petrol) and EVs. Identified that while EV battery manufacturing initially produces higher emissions (10.5 units vs 7.4), EVs are significantly more eco-friendly over their total operational life.
  • Consumer Friction Mapping: Identified three "Major Concerns" currently hindering EV adoption:
    • App Consolidation: Users face "digital clutter," with no single unified platform to manage different charging networks.
    • Range Anxiety: Exacerbated by discrepancies between a vehicle's displayed range and actual performance.
    • Reliability Gaps: Documented the high impact of "broken charging stations," which users often only discover upon arrival.
  • Operational Sensitivity Research: Analyzed how local environmental factors, such as India’s harsh climate and electricity fluctuations, can significantly extend home charging times to an entire day.

The Outcome

The project delivers a clear-eyed roadmap for stakeholders in the EV space. It concludes that while the "Green Narrative" is technically supported by lower long-term emissions, market success depends on solving the "User Experience Paradox". Strategic recommendations include the need for brand collaborations for awareness and the development of consolidated "Super Apps" to manage the charging journey.
